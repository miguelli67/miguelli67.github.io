World War I (1914–1918)

World War I, also known as the Great War, was a global conflict centered in Europe that involved many of the world's great powers.

Causes of WWI

  • Militarism: European nations built up large militaries, leading to an arms race.
  • Alliances: Complex alliances meant that a conflict between two countries could draw in many more.
  • Imperialism: Competition for colonies created tension between powers.
  • Nationalism: Pride and rivalry between nations heightened tensions.
  • Assassination of Archduke Franz Ferdinand: The immediate spark that triggered the war.

Major Events

  • 1914 – War begins after assassination in Sarajevo.
  • 1915 – Sinking of the Lusitania increases tensions with the U.S.
  • 1916 – Battle of the Somme results in massive casualties.
  • 1917 – U.S. enters the war; Russia withdraws after revolution.
  • 1918 – Armistice signed on November 11.

Consequences

  • Over 16 million people died.
  • Political borders in Europe were redrawn.
  • The League of Nations was established to prevent future wars.
  • The Treaty of Versailles placed heavy penalties on Germany, setting the stage for WWII.
