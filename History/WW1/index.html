World War I (1914–1918)

A global conflict centered in Europe, involving complex causes, massive battles, and far-reaching consequences that reshaped the world.

Tap/Click to Reveal the MAIN Causes

M — Militarism 💣
Militarism: Arms races and military buildup made war more likely; leaders trusted force over diplomacy.
A — Alliances 🤝
Alliances: Binding agreements meant a dispute could pull in many nations, turning a spark into a continent-wide war.
I — Imperialism 🌍
Imperialism: Rival empires competed for colonies, resources, and prestige, fueling international tension.
N — Nationalism 🏳️
Nationalism: Intense pride and ethnic aspirations stoked rivalries and independence movements.

Major Pre-War Alliances

Triple Entente: France · Russia · Britain Triple Alliance: Germany · Austria-Hungary · Italy*

*Italy later joined the Allies (1915). The assassination of Archduke Franz Ferdinand (June 1914) triggered a chain reaction through these alliances.

Trench Warfare

What & Why: On the Western Front, both sides dug extensive trenches for protection from machine guns and artillery, creating a deadly stalemate.

  • No Man’s Land: Exposed area between trenches.
  • Over the Top: Infantry charges across No Man’s Land under fire.
  • New Tech: Machine guns, poison gas, tanks, aircraft.

Conditions were harsh: mud, rats, disease, and constant shelling.
