Introduction

Evolution is the process through which species change over time. These changes accumulate and can lead to the formation of new species.

Mechanisms of Evolution

  • Natural Selection: Organisms better adapted to their environment survive and reproduce.
  • Mutation: Changes in DNA that introduce variation.
  • Gene Flow: Movement of genes between populations.
  • Genetic Drift: Random changes in gene frequency in small populations.

Evidence of Evolution

  • Fossil Record
  • Comparative Anatomy
  • Molecular Biology
  • Embryological Similarities

What is Evolution?

Evolution is the change in the characteristics of a species over generations. It relies on the process of natural selection, where certain traits become more common because they help organisms survive and reproduce in their environment.

For example, giraffes with longer necks were better at reaching food high up in trees. Over time, this trait became more common in the population, leading to the giraffes we see today.

Natural Selection

Natural selection is a mechanism by which individuals with traits that are better suited to their environment are more likely to survive and reproduce. These traits are passed down to offspring, and over many generations, can change the traits of the population.

  • Variation: Individuals in a population vary in traits.
  • Adaptation: Some traits provide advantages.
  • Survival: Those with advantageous traits tend to survive and reproduce.
  • Reproduction: Offspring inherit the traits that helped their parents survive.
