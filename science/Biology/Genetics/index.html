What is Genetics?

Genetics is the study of how traits are passed from parents to offspring. It helps us understand heredity, variation, and how DNA carries the blueprint of life.

DNA and Genes

DNA (deoxyribonucleic acid) is a molecule shaped like a double helix. Itâ€™s made up of base pairs (A, T, C, G) and contains the instructions for building proteins. Genes are short sections of DNA that determine specific traits.

Mutations

Mutations are changes in the DNA sequence. They can occur naturally or due to environmental factors like radiation and chemicals. Some mutations have no effect, while others can lead to genetic disorders.

Modern Genetics

Genetic technologies like CRISPR allow scientists to edit genes. The Human Genome Project has mapped all human genes, helping in disease treatment, ancestry research, and more.
