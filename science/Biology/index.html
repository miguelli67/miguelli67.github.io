Prokaryotic vs. Eukaryotic Cells

Prokaryotic cells (like bacteria) do not have a nucleus or membrane-bound organelles. Eukaryotic cells (like plant and animal cells) have a defined nucleus and organelles such as mitochondria and endoplasmic reticulum.

Organelles in Detail

Nucleus

Contains DNA and regulates the cell‚Äôs activities such as growth, metabolism, and reproduction.

Ribosome

Builds proteins by linking amino acids, following genetic instructions from the DNA.

Endoplasmic Reticulum (ER)

Transports proteins and lipids; the rough ER has ribosomes, the smooth ER does not.

Golgi Apparatus

Packages and distributes proteins and lipids inside and outside the cell.

Chloroplast

Found in plant cells; performs photosynthesis to convert sunlight into energy.

Cell Membrane

Protective outer layer that controls what enters and exits the cell.

Mitochondria

Produces ATP through cellular respiration; known as the powerhouse of the cell.

Lysosome

Breaks down waste, old cell parts, and harmful substances using digestive enzymes.
